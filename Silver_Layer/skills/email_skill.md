# Skill: Email

## Purpose
Compose and send professional emails based on vault content and context.

## Inputs
- Recipient: Email address or role
- Subject: Email subject line
- Context: Related files, previous conversations
- Tone: Professional/Friendly/Formal
- Purpose: Inform/Request/Follow-up/Announce

## Outputs
- Email draft with subject and body
- Suggested attachments (if applicable)
- Send timing recommendation
- Follow-up reminder (if needed)

## Process

1. **Understand Context**
   - Read related files and background
   - Identify key information to communicate
   - Determine relationship with recipient
   - Check for previous email threads

2. **Determine Email Structure**
   - **Inform**: Share information, provide updates
   - **Request**: Ask for something, clear CTA
   - **Follow-up**: Reference previous conversation
   - **Announce**: Share news, broader audience

3. **Compose Email**
   - Subject line (clear, specific, under 60 chars)
   - Greeting (appropriate to relationship)
   - Opening (context and purpose)
   - Body (main content, organized)
   - Closing (next steps, CTA)
   - Signature

4. **Apply Tone Guidelines**
   - **Professional**: Clear, respectful, business-focused
   - **Friendly**: Warm, personable, conversational
   - **Formal**: Traditional, structured, conservative

5. **Review Checklist**
   - Subject line is clear and specific
   - Purpose is stated early
   - Content is concise and organized
   - Call-to-action is clear (if applicable)
   - Tone matches relationship
   - No typos or errors

## Success Criteria
- Email clearly communicates purpose
- Tone is appropriate for recipient
- Length is appropriate (not too long)
- Call-to-action is clear
- Professional formatting

## Error Handling
- **Missing recipient**: Request clarification
- **Unclear purpose**: Ask for more context
- **Sensitive content**: Flag for human review
- **Urgent timing**: Highlight and expedite

## Email Best Practices
- Keep subject lines under 60 characters
- State purpose in first 2 sentences
- Use bullet points for readability
- One email = one topic
- Include clear call-to-action
- Proofread before sending
- Consider timing (business hours)
- Follow up if no response in 3-5 days
